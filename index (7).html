About Solstice

A small firm, built for decisive moments.

We work with a deliberately small roster of clients at a time — usually companies between 20 and 400 people who've hit a point where the old way of operating no longer fits the business they've become.

Founded in 2012, Solstice Advisory pairs strategic clarity with the unglamorous work of implementation: org design, process redesign, and the reporting rhythms that make a plan stick past the workshop that produced it.

  • Independent by design

    We take no referral fees and hold no equity in client businesses, so our recommendations serve you, not a downstream relationship.

  • In the operations, not just the slides

    Every engagement includes implementation support — we stay through the first two quarters of execution, not just the planning phase.

  • Sized to the problem

    Our teams run two to four consultants per engagement. No armies of juniors, no hand-offs you didn't agree to.

Questions

Frequently asked questions.

How long does a typical engagement run?+

Most engagements run twelve to twenty weeks: four to six weeks of diagnosis and planning, followed by implementation support through the first stretch of execution.

Do you work with early-stage startups?+

Occasionally, but our practice is built around companies with an established operating history — usually 20 to 400 employees — where there's an existing structure to redesign rather than one to invent from scratch.

What industries do you specialize in?+

We work across professional services, logistics, and B2B software. The common thread is operating complexity, not sector — if growth has outpaced your systems, that's the fit we look for.

How is pricing structured?+

Engagements are scoped and quoted as a fixed project fee, agreed before work begins. We don't bill hourly, and we don't extend scope without a conversation first.

Can you work with our existing leadership team rather than around them?+

Yes — in fact we insist on it. Our engagements are built to run alongside your leadership team, not in a side room, so the plan belongs to your company once we leave.
